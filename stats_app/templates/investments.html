Monte Carlo Simulation

Monte Carlo simulation is like playing a game of chance, but instead of rolling dice or spinning a wheel, we use computers to simulate many possible outcomes for a situation. Imagine you're planning your finances for the next year, but there are many unpredictable factors like stock market changes or business trends. With Monte Carlo simulation, we create 252 different scenarios, each representing a possible future for your portfolio over the next 252 business days. We do this by randomly selecting values for these unpredictable factors based on their historical behavior. By running these simulations, we can see a range of possible outcomes for your portfolio's performance. It's like looking at 252 different paths your finances could take, helping you make more informed decisions and prepare for different possibilities.
